Info

Explore more about coal.

  • Anthracite

    The highest rank of coal, with a high percentage of carbon
    and low percentage of volatile matter. It's hard, brittle, and black.

  • bituminous

    A middle rank coal with a high heating value. It's blocky, shiny,
    and smooth, but may have thin layers that alternate between shiny and dull.

  • sub-bituminous

    A black coal with low-to-moderate heating values.
    It's mainly used for electricity generation.

  • lignite

    Lignite is a soft, brown, combustible sedimentary rock that is the lowest grade of coal
